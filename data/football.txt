Football Knowledge Base

Brazil has won the FIFA World Cup a record five times, in 1958, 1962, 1970, 1994, and 2002.
The first FIFA World Cup was held in Uruguay in 1930, and was won by the host nation.
Lionel Messi won the FIFA World Cup with Argentina in 2022, defeating France on penalties in the final.
Cristiano Ronaldo is one of the top goal scorers in football history across club and international competitions.
Germany has won the World Cup four times, most recently in 2014, defeating Argentina in the final.
The FIFA World Cup is held every four years and is the most widely viewed sporting event in the world.
Pele won three World Cup titles with Brazil, in 1958, 1962, and 1970, and remains a symbol of Brazilian football excellence.
The UEFA Champions League is Europe's premier club football competition, contested annually by top clubs from European domestic leagues.
Real Madrid has won the UEFA Champions League a record number of times among European clubs.
The Ballon d'Or is an annual award given to the player considered to have performed best over the previous year.
France won the FIFA World Cup in 2018, defeating Croatia in the final held in Russia.
The offside rule states that an attacking player is offside if they are nearer to the opponent's goal line than both the ball and the second-last opponent when the ball is played to them.
