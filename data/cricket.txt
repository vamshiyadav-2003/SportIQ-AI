Cricket Knowledge Base

Rohit Sharma holds the record for the highest individual score in ODI cricket, scoring 264 runs against Sri Lanka in 2014 at Kolkata.
Sachin Tendulkar is the highest run-scorer in international cricket history, with over 34,000 runs across Test, ODI, and T20I formats combined.
Virat Kohli has scored more ODI centuries than any other batsman in history.
Muttiah Muralitharan is the leading wicket-taker in Test cricket history, with 800 wickets.
Australia has won the most Cricket World Cup titles, lifting the trophy in 1987, 1999, 2003, 2007, and 2015.
India won the 2011 Cricket World Cup on home soil, defeating Sri Lanka in the final at Mumbai.
Australia defeated India in the final of the 2023 Cricket World Cup, held in India.
The fastest century in ODI cricket was scored by AB de Villiers, in just 31 balls, against West Indies in 2015.
Brian Lara holds the record for the highest individual score in Test cricket, with 400 runs (not out) against England in 2004.
The T20 World Cup was first held in 2007, and was won by India, defeating Pakistan in the final.
MS Dhoni is widely regarded as one of the greatest captains in Indian cricket history, leading India to the 2007 T20 World Cup, 2011 ODI World Cup, and 2013 Champions Trophy titles.
Jasprit Bumrah is known for his unorthodox bowling action and has been ranked among the top fast bowlers in world cricket.
The Duckworth-Lewis-Stern (DLS) method is used to calculate target scores in rain-affected limited-overs matches.
