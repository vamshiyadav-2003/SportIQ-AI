Tennis Knowledge Base

The four Grand Slam tournaments in tennis are the Australian Open, French Open (Roland Garros), Wimbledon, and the US Open.
Novak Djokovic holds the record for the most Grand Slam singles titles won by a male player.
Serena Williams won 23 Grand Slam singles titles during her career, the most by any player in the Open Era.
Rafael Nadal is nicknamed the "King of Clay" for his dominance at the French Open, where he won the title a record number of times.
Wimbledon is the oldest tennis tournament in the world, first held in 1877, and is the only Grand Slam played on grass courts.
Roger Federer, Rafael Nadal, and Novak Djokovic are collectively known as the "Big Three" of men's tennis for their dominance over two decades.
The Australian Open and US Open are played on hard courts, while the French Open is played on clay and Wimbledon on grass.
A tennis match is won by winning sets, and a set is typically won by being the first to win six games with a margin of at least two games.
Tie-breaks are used to decide a set when the score reaches six games apiece, in most tournaments.
Steffi Graf is the only player, male or female, to complete a "Golden Slam" - winning all four Grand Slams and an Olympic gold medal in the same calendar year, in 1988.
