Volleyball Knowledge Base & Historical Facts

1. Volleyball is a team sport in which two teams of six players are separated by a net, aiming to score points by grounding a ball on the opponent's court.
2. The game was invented in February 1895 by William G. Morgan, a physical education director at the YMCA in Holyoke, Massachusetts, USA.
3. Morgan originally named the sport "Mintonette" because of its similarity to badminton.
4. Mintonette was designed to be an indoor sport for older members of the YMCA who found basketball too vigorous and exhausting.
5. The name "volleyball" was adopted in 1896 after Alfred Halstead, a spectator at the first exhibition match, noticed that the players were volleying the ball back and forth over the net.
6. The first official rules of volleyball were published in the YMCA physical education handbook in 1897.
7. Initially, the game was played with an unlimited number of players on each side, and the net was 6 feet 6 inches (1.98 meters) high.
8. The first volleyballs were custom-designed in 1900 by Spalding at the request of William G. Morgan, as basketballs were too heavy and soccer ball bladders were too soft.
9. In 1916, the YMCA and the National Collegiate Athletic Association (NCAA) jointly issued a set of rules, which included introducing the "set and spike" offensive tactic in the Philippines.
10. The rule limiting a team to three touches of the ball before hitting it over the net was established in 1920.
11. The rotation rule, where players rotate clockwise to change positions and servers, was introduced in 1912 to ensure all players played both defense and offense.
12. The Fédération Internationale de Volleyball (FIVB), the international governing body for the sport, was founded in Paris, France, in April 1947.
13. Paul Libaud of France was the first president of the FIVB, serving for 37 years from 1947 to 1984.
14. The FIVB is currently headquartered in Lausanne, Switzerland.
15. The first FIVB Men's World Championship was held in Prague, Czechoslovakia, in 1949, and was won by the Soviet Union.
16. The first FIVB Women's World Championship was held in Moscow, Soviet Union, in 1952, also won by the Soviet Union.
17. Volleyball was introduced as an official Olympic sport for both men and women at the 1964 Tokyo Olympics.
18. Japan won the women's gold medal at the 1964 Tokyo Olympics, earning the nickname "Witches of the Orient" for their incredible defensive diving and speed.
19. The Soviet Union won the men's gold medal at the 1964 Tokyo Olympics.
20. The United States Men's Olympic Volleyball Team won back-to-back gold medals at the 1984 Los Angeles Olympics and 1988 Seoul Olympics.
21. Karch Kiraly of the USA is widely regarded as the greatest volleyball player of all time.
22. Kiraly is the only player to win Olympic gold medals in both indoor volleyball (1984, 1988) and beach volleyball (1996).
23. Kiraly also coached the United States Women's National Team to their first-ever Olympic gold medal at the 2020 Tokyo Olympics.
24. Beach volleyball developed in the 1920s in Santa Monica, California, as a recreational activity before becoming a global competitive sport.
25. Beach volleyball became an official Olympic sport at the 1996 Atlanta Olympics.
26. Misty May-Treanor and Kerri Walsh Jennings of the USA are the most successful beach volleyball duo in history, winning three consecutive Olympic gold medals (2004, 2008, 2012).
27. A standard indoor volleyball court is rectangular, measuring 18 meters (59 feet) long by 9 meters (29.5 feet) wide.
28. The court is divided into two halves of 9x9 meters by the net.
29. The "attack line" (or 3-meter line) is located 3 meters (9.8 feet) away from the net on each side, separating the front row from the back row.
30. The height of the net for men's competition is set at 2.43 meters (7 feet 11 5/8 inches).
31. The height of the net for women's competition is set at 2.24 meters (7 feet 4 1/8 inches).
32. The net is 1 meter wide and has two vertical antennas attached to the sides directly above the court sidelines.
33. The ball must pass entirely between the two antennas when hit over the net; if it hits or passes outside the antennas, it is ruled out of bounds.
34. A standard indoor volleyball is made of leather or synthetic leather, has a circumference of 65-67 centimeters, and weighs 260-280 grams.
35. The "rally scoring system" was introduced by the FIVB in 1999, replacing the side-out scoring system where teams could only score when serving.
36. Under the current system, a match is played as the best of 5 sets. The first four sets are played to 25 points, and the team must win by a 2-point lead.
37. If a fifth set (tiebreaker) is necessary, it is played to 15 points, and the team must still win by a 2-point lead.
38. A team is allowed a maximum of three hits to return the ball over the net, in addition to a block touch.
39. The same player cannot hit the ball twice in succession (except if the first touch was a block, which does not count as one of the three team hits).
40. The "Libero" position was introduced in 1998 to improve defensive play. The Libero wears a different colored jersey from their teammates.
41. The Libero is a defensive specialist who plays only in the back row and cannot serve, spike the ball above net height, or block.
42. Substitutions involving the Libero do not count against the team's standard substitution limit, and they can enter and exit the court freely between rallies.
43. A "serve" is the act of putting the ball into play by hitting it from behind the end line over the net into the opponent's court.
44. The "underhand serve" is a basic serve where the ball is hit from below waist level, commonly used by beginners.
45. The "floater serve" is an overhand serve hit without spin, causing the ball's path to be erratic and unpredictable, similar to a knuckleball in baseball.
46. The "jump serve" is an advanced serve where the player tosses the ball high, runs, jumps, and spikes it, generating high speed and power.
47. The "spike" (or attack) is the act of jumping and hitting the ball forcefully downwards into the opponent's court, usually executed by front-row players.
48. The "set" is a tactical pass, usually made with the fingertips high above the head, that positions the ball for a spiker to attack.
49. The "bump" (or forearm pass) is a defensive technique where a player uses their forearms to pass the ball, typically used to receive serves or hard spikes.
50. The "block" is a defensive action executed close to the net by jumping and reaching above the net to redirect an opponent's spike back into their court.
51. A "block touch" does not count as one of the team's three hits; the team still has three hits remaining to return the ball.
52. A "dig" is the act of preventing a spiked ball from hitting the court floor, often requiring players to dive or roll.
53. The "pancake" is a spectacular defensive save where a player slides their hand flat on the floor under the ball just before it hits the ground, causing it to bounce off the back of the hand.
54. A "joust" occurs when two opposing players touch the ball simultaneously above the net. Play continues after a joust, and the team on whose side the ball falls has three new hits.
55. A "double hit" is a violation that occurs when a player hits the ball twice in succession or the ball contacts multiple parts of their body sequentially.
56. A "carry" (or lift/catch) is a violation where the ball is caught, held, or thrown instead of being cleanly hit.
57. The "foot fault" occurs when a server steps on or over the end line during the service contact, resulting in an immediate point for the opponent.
58. A "net violation" occurs when a player contacts the net with any part of their body or clothing during an active play or block.
59. Players in the back row cannot spike the ball from inside the attack zone (front court) if the ball is entirely above the top of the net. They must jump from behind the 3-meter line.
60. The FIVB World Grand Champions Cup was created in 1993, held every four years in the year following the Olympics, featuring continental champions.
61. The Volleyball Nations League (VNL) was launched in 2018, replacing the World League (men) and World Grand Prix (women) as the premier annual global tournament.
62. Brazil is one of the most successful nations in modern volleyball, winning three men's Olympic gold medals (1992, 2004, 2016) and two women's gold medals (2008, 2012).
63. Italy has had a highly successful men's national team, nicknamed "The Generation of Phenomena", which dominated the World Championships in the 1990s by winning three consecutive titles (1990, 1994, 1998).
64. Giba of Brazil is considered one of the most charismatic and dominant outside hitters of the 2000s, winning the 2004 Olympic gold and three World Championships.
65. Lang Ping of China is a volleyball legend, winning Olympic gold as a player in 1984 and as a coach of the Chinese women's team in 2016.
66. Lang Ping is the first person in volleyball history to win Olympic gold as both a player and a head coach.
67. In beach volleyball, teams consist of only two players, with no substitutions allowed.
68. A beach volleyball court is slightly smaller than an indoor court, measuring 16x8 meters, and is covered with deep sand.
69. There is no Libero in beach volleyball, and players do not have fixed positions on the court; they do not rotate positions, only servers change.
70. A beach volleyball match is the best of 3 sets. The first two sets are played to 21 points, and the third set (if needed) is played to 15 points.
71. In beach volleyball, tipping the ball with open fingers (a dink) is a violation; players must use their knuckles or a closed hand to play soft shots.
72. In beach volleyball, teams change sides of the court every 7 points during the first two sets, and every 5 points in the third set, to equalize the effects of wind and sun.
73. A "pipe" attack is an offensive play where a back-row player spikes the ball after it is set in the middle of the court, jumping from behind the attack line.
74. The "setter" is the playmaker of the team, responsible for directing the offense and choosing which teammate will attack.
75. The "opposite hitter" is the player who plays opposite the setter, usually serving as the team's primary offensive scorer from both the front and back rows.
76. The "outside hitter" (or left-side hitter) attacks from the left side of the court and is responsible for both hitting and passing/defending.
77. The "middle blocker" is usually the tallest player on the team, specializing in blocking at the net and executing fast, quick attacks near the setter.
78. A "quick set" is a low, extremely fast set where the middle blocker is already in the air before the setter releases the ball, catching the defense off guard.
79. The "slide" is an attack where the player runs behind the setter and spikes the ball off one foot, commonly used in women's volleyball.
80. A "line shot" is a spike directed straight down the sideline of the opponent's court.
81. A "cross-court shot" (or angle shot) is a spike directed diagonally across the court.
82. A "tool" (or wipe/block-out) is an intentional spike aimed at the blocker's hands so that the ball deflects out of bounds, scoring a point for the attacking team.
83. A "free ball" occurs when the opponent is forced to pass the ball softly over the net instead of spiking it, giving the receiving team an easy opportunity to build an attack.
84. A team can commit a rotation violation if players are out of their correct positions on the court at the moment the server hits the ball.
85. The server has 8 seconds to hit the ball after the referee blows the whistle for service.
86. If the ball touches the ceiling or any overhead obstruction and lands on the same side of the court, it remains in play (if the team has hits left). If it goes over the net after hitting the ceiling, it is a fault.
87. A match referee is assisted by a second referee (standing opposite near the net), a scorer, and two or four line judges.
88. The FIVB introduced a video challenge system in 2012 to review referee decisions, including ball in/out, net touch, and block touch.
89. In indoor volleyball, if the ball hits the net on a serve but still crosses over and lands in the opponent's court, it is ruled a live ball. This "let serve" rule was adopted in 1999.
90. The first US Open Volleyball Championship was held in 1928, organized by the newly formed United States Volleyball Association (USVBA, now USA Volleyball).
91. Cuba's women's national team, known as the "Spectacular Caribbean Girls", dominated the 1990s by winning three consecutive Olympic gold medals (1992, 1996, 2000).
92. Regla Torres of Cuba was named the "Best Female Player of the 20th Century" by the FIVB at age 25.
93. Ron Zwerver of the Netherlands led the Dutch men's team to a gold medal at the 1996 Atlanta Olympics, famous for their high-tempo "Oranje" style of play.
94. The "screen" is an illegal action where serving team players wave hands or stand close together to block the opponent's view of the server or the ball's path.
95. A "dump" is a surprise offensive play where the setter, instead of setting the ball for a teammate, tips it directly over the net on the second touch.
96. The "six-pack" (or facial) is a slang term in volleyball that occurs when a player is hit directly in the face or head by a hard-driven spike.
97. A "yellow card" is given by the referee as a formal warning for minor misconduct.
98. A "red card" is issued for rude conduct, resulting in a penalty where the opponent receives a point and service.
99. A combination of yellow and red cards held in one hand indicates expulsion from the set; holding them in separate hands indicates disqualification from the match.
100. Volleyball is played by over 800 million people worldwide, making it one of the most widely played sports in existence.
